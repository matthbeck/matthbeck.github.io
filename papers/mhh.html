A Shorter, Simpler, Stronger Proof of the Meshalkin-Hochberg-Hirsch Bounds on Componentwise Antichains

Matthias Beck and Thomas Zaslavsky

Abstract. Meshalkin's theorem states that a class of ordered p-partitions of an n-set has at most max \binom{n}{a_1,..., a_p} members if for each k the kth parts form an antichain. We give a new proof of this and the corresponding LYM inequality due to Hochberg and Hirsch, which is simpler and more general than previous proofs. It extends to a common generalization of Meshalkin's theorem and Erdos's theorem about r-chain-free set families.
