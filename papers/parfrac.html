The partial-fractions method for counting solutions to integral linear systems

Matthias Beck

Abstract. We present a new tool to compute the number phiA(b) of nonnegative integer solutions x to

A x = b ,

where the coefficients of A and b are integral. phiA(b) is often described as a vector partition function. Our methods use partial fraction expansions of Euler's generating function for phiA(b). Applications include Ehrhart (quasi-)polynomials counting integer points in dilated polytopes, their multivariate analogs (exemplified through transportation polytopes), and Littlewood-Richardson coefficients appearing in symmetric functions and tensor multiplicities.
