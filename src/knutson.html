Allen Knutson

University of California, Berkeley

Toric simplicial complexes

Abstract for the Joint Summer Research Conference "Integer points in polyhedra"

Given a triangulation of a lattice polytope, one can reduce the question of counting lattice points in the polytope to that of counting lattice points in the individual simplices. In the toric variety setting, this corresponds to a degeneration of the toric variety to a ``toric simplicial complex''. Amazingly, the work of Littelmann and Chirivi give a degeneration of each flag manifold to a toric simplicial complex, giving multiplicity formulae again in terms of counting lattice points in simplices. I'll explain a common framework that produces both of these degenerations, and should lead to many others.
