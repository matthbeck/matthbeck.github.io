Andrei Zelevinsky

Northeastern University

Tropical calculus: recent applications

Abstract for the Joint Summer Research Conference "Integer points in polyhedra"

The ``tropical product" of two real numbers is their usual sum, while the ``tropical sum" is their minimum. This formalism establishes a link between piecewise-linear functions and subtraction-free rational expressions. Its first uses in the 1970's were in control theory and combinatorial optimization. We will discuss two recent applications of a completely different kind:

1) expressing tensor product multiplicities for semisimple groups as numbers of lattice points in some convex polytopes; and

2) proving Alexey Zamolodchikov's periodicity conjecture for Y-systems arising in thermodynamic Bethe ansatz.

The talk will be elementary and will require practically no background from representation theory or physics.
