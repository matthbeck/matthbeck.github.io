Proposition 3.3.3.

The map \(\phi\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \phi(x,y,z) = \begin{cases}\left(\frac{x}{1-z}, \, \frac{y}{1-z},\, 0\right) \amp \text{ if } z \ne 1 \, , \\ \infty \amp \text{ if } z=1 \, . \end{cases} \end{equation*}
It is bijective, with inverse map
\begin{equation*} \phi^{-1}(p,q,0) \ = \ \left(\frac{2p}{p^2+q^2+1},\, \frac{2q}{p^2+q^2+1}, \, \frac{p^2+q^2-1}{p^2+q^2+1}\right) \end{equation*}
and \(\phi^{-1}(\infty) = (0,0,1)\text{.}\)
