Proposition 9.1.10.

Suppose \(z_0\) is an isolated singularity of \(f\) with Laurent series
\begin{equation*} f(z) \ = \ \sz c_k ( z - z_0 )^k\text{,} \end{equation*}
valid in some punctured disk centered at \(z_0\text{.}\) Then
  1. \(z_0\) is removable if and only if there are no negative exponents (that is, the Laurent series is a power series);

Proof.

  1. Suppose \(z_0\) is removable. Then there exists a holomorphic function \(g: D[z_0,R] \to \C\) that agrees with \(f\) on \(\Dp[z_0,R]\text{,}\) for some \(R > 0\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 8.1.8, \(g\) has a power series expansion centered at \(z_0\text{,}\) which coincides with the Laurent series of \(f\) at \(z_0\text{,}\) by CorollaryÂ 8.3.7.

    Conversely, if the Laurent series of \(f\) at \(z_0\) has only nonnegative powers, we can use it to define a function that is holomorphic at \(z_0\text{.}\)

  2. Suppose \(z_0\) is a pole of order \(n\text{.}\) Then, by CorollaryÂ 9.1.7, \(f(z)=(z-z_0)^{-n}g(z)\) on some punctured disk \(\Dp[z_0,R]\text{,}\) where \(g\) is holomorphic on \(D[z_0,R]\) and \(g(z_0)\ne0\text{.}\) Thus \(g(z)=\sum_{k\ge0}c_k(z-z_0)^k\) in \(D[z_0,R]\) with \(c_0\ne0\text{,}\) so
    \begin{equation*} f(z)=(z-z_0)^{-n}\sum_{k\ge0}c_k(z-z_0)^k = \sum_{k\ge-n}c_{k+n}(z-z_0)^k\, \text{,} \end{equation*}
    and this is the Laurent series of \(f\text{,}\) by CorollaryÂ 8.3.7.

    Conversely, suppose that
    \begin{align*} f(z) \amp \ = \ \sum_{k \geq -n} c_k ( z - z_0 )^k\\ \amp \ = \ (z-z_0)^{-n} \sum_{k \geq -n} c_k ( z - z_0 )^{k+n}\\ \amp \ = \ (z-z_0)^{-n} \s c_{k-n} (z-z_0)^k \end{align*}
    where \(c_{-n} \not= 0\text{.}\) Define \(g(z) \ := \ \s c_{k-n} (z-z_0)^k\text{.}\) Then \(g\) is holomorphic at \(z_0\) and \(g(z_0) = c_{-n}\ne0\) so, by CorollaryÂ 9.1.7, \(f\) has a pole of order \(n\) at \(z_0\text{.}\)
