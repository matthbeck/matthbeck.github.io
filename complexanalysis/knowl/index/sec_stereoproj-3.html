To begin, we think of \(\C\) as the \((x,y)\)-plane in \(\R^3\text{,}\) that is, \(\C = \{(x,y,0) \in \R^3\}\text{.}\) To describe stereographic projection, we will be less concerned with actual complex numbers \(x + iy\) and more concerned with their coordinates. Consider the unit sphere
\begin{equation*} \mathbb{S}^2 \ := \ \left\{(x,y,z) \in \R^3 : \, x^2+y^2+z^2=1 \right\} \, \text{.} \end{equation*}
The sphere and the complex plane intersect in the set \(\{(x,y,0) : \, x^2 + y^2 = 1\}\text{,}\) which corresponds to the equator on the sphere and the unit circle on the complex plane. Let \(N:=(0,0,1)\text{,}\) the north pole of \(\mathbb{S}^2\text{,}\) and let \(S:= (0,0,-1)\text{,}\) the south pole.
