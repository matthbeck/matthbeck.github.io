Theorem A.0.3. Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.

Suppose \(f: [a,b] \to \R\) is continuous.
  1. The function \(F: [a,b] \to \R\) defined by \(F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\,\diff{t}\) is differentiable and \(F'(x)=f(x)\text{.}\)
